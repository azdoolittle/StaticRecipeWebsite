  • Filipino Chicken Adobo

    Philippine adobo is a popular Filipino dish and cooking process in Philippine cuisine that involves meat, seafood, or vegetables marinated in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and black peppercorns, which is browned in oil, and simmered in the marinade. It has occasionally been considered the unofficial national dish in the Philippines.

  • Ingredients
    1. 1 (3 pound) chicken, cut into pieces
    2. 1 large onion, quartered and sliced
    3. 2 tablespoons minced garlic
    4. ⅓ cup white vinegar
    5. ⅔ cup low sodium soy sauce
    6. 1 tablespoon garlic powder
    7. 2 teaspoons black pepper
    8. 1 bay leaf
  • Recipe
    1. Combine the chicken thighs, vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, peppercorns and bay leaves in a large pot. Cover and marinate the chicken in the refrigerator for 1 to 3 hours.
    2. Bring the chicken to a boil over high heat. Lower the heat, cover and let simmer for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally. Remove the lid and simmer until the sauce is reduced and thickened and the chicken is tender, about 20 more minutes. Serve with steamed rice.
